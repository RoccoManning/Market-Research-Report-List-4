<p><p>Microwave circulators are devices that control the direction of microwave signals in a transmission line. The three main types are passive circulators, which do not require external power; three-port circulators, which have one input and two output ports; and four-port circulators, which have one input and three output ports. Passive circulators are simple and cost-effective, while three-port circulators are commonly used in wireless communication systems. Four-port circulators offer more versatile signal routing options for more complex applications.</p></p>
