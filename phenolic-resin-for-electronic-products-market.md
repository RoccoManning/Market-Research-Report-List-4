<p><p>Phenolic resin is commonly used in electronic products due to its excellent properties such as heat resistance, chemical resistance, and electrical insulation. There are two main types of phenolic resin used in the market for electronic products: thermoplastic phenolic resin and thermosetting phenolic resin. Thermoplastic phenolic resin can be melted and reformed multiple times, making it ideal for applications requiring flexibility. In contrast, thermosetting phenolic resin undergoes a chemical reaction during curing, resulting in a rigid and durable material that is well-suited for high-temperature environments in electronic devices.</p></p>
