<p><p>Carbon steel rods are categorized into two main market types based on their carbon content: Low Carbon Steel and High Carbon Steel. Low Carbon Steel rods have a carbon content of 0.05-0.30%, making them more ductile and easily weldable. They are commonly used in construction and manufacturing applications. On the other hand, High Carbon Steel rods have a carbon content of 0.30-2.0%, making them harder and more brittle. They are often used in tools, blades, and automotive parts that require high strength and wear resistance.</p></p>
