<p><p>Dental extraoral X-rays are divided into two main types: panoramic and Cone Beam Computed Tomography (CBCT). Panoramic X-rays provide a wide view of the entire mouth area, including the teeth, jawbone, and surrounding structures. They are commonly used for orthodontic treatment and evaluating the overall dental health of a patient. On the other hand, CBCT X-rays provide a more detailed, three-dimensional image of a specific area of the mouth, making them ideal for precise diagnosis and treatment planning for complex dental issues.</p></p>
