<p><p>Motorized spine operating tables are equipped with electrical motors that allow for precise adjustments in height, tilt, and lateral movement. These tables offer enhanced control and convenience for surgeons during spinal procedures. </p><p>Non-motorized spine operating tables, on the other hand, rely on manual adjustments made by the surgical team. While they may require more physical effort to position patients, they are often more cost-effective and simpler to maintain. Both types of tables play a crucial role in providing optimal patient positioning and accessibility during spinal surgeries.</p></p>
