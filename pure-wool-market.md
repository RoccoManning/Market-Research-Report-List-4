<p><p>The pure wool market consists of various types of wool sourced from different animals. Sheep's wool is the most common and widely used type, known for its versatility and durability. Merino wool is prized for its softness and moisture-wicking properties. Mohair wool comes from Angora goats and is known for its luster and warmth. Angora wool is sourced from Angora rabbits and is known for its softness and luxurious feel. Cashmere wool is obtained from cashmere goats and is highly prized for its softness and warmth. Alpaca wool comes from Alpacas and is known for its softness and hypoallergenic properties. Other types of wool include camel hair, llama wool, and yak wool. Each type of wool offers unique characteristics making them suitable for different products and applications in the textile industry.</p></p>
