<p><p>Inherent fabric in firefighting protective clothing is made from materials that have natural heat and flame resistance properties without the need for chemical treatments. This type of fabric is durable and maintains its fire-resistant properties throughout the life of the garment. Treated fabric, on the other hand, is made from materials that are treated with chemical coatings to enhance their flame resistance. While treated fabrics may offer cost savings, they may lose their fire-resistant properties over time with wear and washing.</p></p>
