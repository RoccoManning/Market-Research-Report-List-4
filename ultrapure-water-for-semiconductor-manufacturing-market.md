<p><p>Ultrapure water for semiconductor manufacturing can be classified into two types based on its electrical resistance: above 18 Ω.cm and below 18 Ω.cm. Water with a resistance above 18 Ω.cm is considered high purity and is typically used in critical cleaning processes and rinsing applications. On the other hand, water with a resistance below 18 Ω.cm is ultra-high purity and is commonly used in advanced semiconductor manufacturing processes where even the smallest impurities can cause device failures.</p></p>
