<p><p>Activated charcoal fiber is categorized into various types based on the raw material used for its production. Pitch-based activated charcoal fiber is derived from coal tar or petroleum pitch. Polyacrylonitrile (PAN) based activated charcoal fiber is produced from a synthetic polymer. Viscose staple based activated charcoal fiber is made from cellulose. Other types include those made from bamboo or coconut shells. Each type has unique properties and applications in industries such as healthcare, environmental protection, and personal care products.</p></p>
